Class Description

The course will start with an introductory section covering general concepts about cyber security, followed by research seminars where recent papers from leading conferences will be presented by students or the instructor. I expect each student to read each paper before class and actively participate in the discussion of all papers. In addition, there will be a semester-long class project. Students can work individually, or in a team of two members. Each group is expected to write a conference-style paper describing their work. There will also be several security labs. Students will gain hands-on experience with common security vulnerabilities in network applications through these labs. Paper presentation will be around 45 minutes, including about 35-40 minutes of presentation and 5-10 minutes of discussion. Before each class, we use the discussion section in Canvas to post comments about the paper to be presented, which is like a mini-review for the paper. One student of the group who present the paper should use the paper title as the title for the discussion thread. Other students use this thread to post their comments. Please don't copy and paste text from the paper.

Grading

Paper summary 10%
Presentation 8%
Attack of the Week Presentation 2%
Class attendance 10%
Lab assignment 30%
Semester-long Class project 40%
Final score sum up the points obtained from each category, letter grade (A, A-, B+, …)

Presentation grades will be based on: 1) the depth in presentation (i.e., how in-depth the paper is understood, correctness of responses to questions from the audience); and 2) slides preparation (slides need to be informative with technical depth). Class project grades will be assigned based on the novelty of the design, completeness of the system implementation, and clarity in the report.

Grading Policies

All assignments should be submitted through the course website (Canvas), unless stated otherwise. Late submissions are highly discouraged. An immediate deduction of 50% will be applied to submissions after the deadline but before 24 hours past the deadline; submissions made 24 hours or more past the deadline will not be accepted. Late submissions for the project proposal and report will not be accepted. These rules will be strictly enforced unless stated otherwise by the instructor. If you feel that an error has been made in grading an assignment or an exam, you must submit a written re-grade petition within one week after the assignment or exam has been returned to you. Verbal appeals are not acceptable and grades will not be changed after the one-week period.
